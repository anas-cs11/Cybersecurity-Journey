# History of Cybersecurity & Early Threats
Cybersecurity evolved as computers and networks became more common, leading to new risks and the need to protect sensitive information. Early security focused on basic protection, but over time, threats became more advanced and widespread, targeting both systems and human behavior.
## PII & SPII
Personally Identifiable Information (PII) refers to any data that can identify an individual.
### PII Examples
- Name
- Email address
- Phone number  
Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information (SPII) is a more critical form of PII that requires stronger protection.
### SPII Examples
- Aadhaar / SSN
- Bank details
- Passwords  
## Early Malware Concepts
Malware refers to malicious software designed to harm or exploit systems.
### Types
- Virus → attaches to files and spreads when executed
- Worm → spreads automatically across networks
- Malware → general term for harmful software  
## Historical Attacks
- Brain Virus (1986) → first known PC virus
- Morris Worm (1988) → first major internet worm causing disruption  
## Modern vs Old Attacks
### Old Attacks
- Limited spread
- Focused on systems
- Less complex  
### Modern Attacks
- Large-scale and automated
- Target both humans and systems
- Use advanced techniques and tools  
## Common Modern Threats
- Social Engineering → manipulating people
- Phishing → fake emails/messages to steal data
- Vulnerabilities → weaknesses in systems  
## Real-World Example
- Equifax Breach → exposed sensitive data of millions due to unpatched vulnerability
