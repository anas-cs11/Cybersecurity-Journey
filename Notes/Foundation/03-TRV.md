# Threats, Risks, Vulnerabilities & Security Practices
## Introduction
In cybersecurity, understanding threats, risks, and vulnerabilities is essential to protect systems effectively.These concepts help identify what can go wrong, how it can happen, and what impact it may cause.
## Threats
A threat is any potential danger that can exploit a system.
- Examples:
- Malware
- Phishing attacks
- Insider threats
## Vulnerabilities
A vulnerability is a weakness in a system that can be exploited by a threat.
- Examples:
- Outdated software
- Weak passwords
- Misconfigured systems
## Risks
Risk is the potential damage or loss when a threat exploits a vulnerability.
- Simple idea:  Risk = Threat + Vulnerability + Impact
## Relationship Between Them
Threats exploit vulnerabilities, which leads to risks.Understanding this relationship helps in building strong security defenses.
## Cybersecurity Frameworks
Frameworks provide structured guidelines to manage and improve security.
- Examples:
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework
- ISO 27001
They help organizations identify, protect, detect, respond, and recover from threats.
## Security Design Principles
Security should be built into systems from the beginning.
- Key principles:
- Least privilege → give minimum access
- Defense in depth → multiple layers of security
- Zero trust → never trust, always verify
## Ethics in Cybersecurity
Ethics guide professionals to act responsibly and legally.
- Key points:
- Protect user privacy
- Avoid misuse of data
- Follow laws and regulations
- Act with integrity and responsibility
