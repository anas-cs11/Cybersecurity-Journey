## The Shift from On-Premise to Cloud Networking

Traditionally, companies owned and physically stored their network devices—like routers, switches, and servers—in their own office buildings. Today, many are migrating to third-party providers to save money and increase flexibility.

**What is Cloud Computing?**

- **Definition:** The practice of using remote servers, applications, and network services hosted on the internet instead of on local, physical devices.
- **The "Cloud" Location:** A collection of servers and computers that store data in a remote data center. These are accessed via the internet rather than a physical cable in your office.

| **Feature** | **Traditional (On-Premise)** | **Cloud Network** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Location** | Physical office or building. | Remote data centers. |
| **Management** | Handled by the company's internal **IT (Information Technology)** team. | Managed by a third-party **CSP (Cloud Service Provider)**. |
| **Accessibility** | Often limited to the physical site. | Accessible from any geographic location. |
| **Cost Model** | High upfront costs for hardware. | Pay-as-you-go (On-demand). |

**The Benefits of Cloud Migration**

Organizations are moving to the cloud for three primary reasons:

1. **Cost Reduction:** Companies save money by not buying, housing, or powering their own servers.
2. **Scalability:** Providers offer **on-demand** processing power. You only pay for what you use, and you can add more resources instantly as your business grows.
3. **Analytics:** **CSPs (Cloud Service Providers)** provide built-in web and business analytics to monitor traffic and sales automatically.

---

**Security in the Cloud: A New Focus**

In a traditional network, security is mostly "network-based" (defending the physical office perimeter). In the cloud, the focus shifts toward **Identity-Based Security**.

- **Network-Based Security:** Verifying **where** the traffic is coming from (using IP addresses or secure tunnels).
- **Identity-Based Security:** Verifying **who** is accessing the data. Because users can log in from a home office, a coffee shop, or another country, the "identity" of the person becomes the new security boundary.

**Real-World Case Study: The Remote Workforce**
Imagine a company that moves its file storage to the cloud.

- **The Scenario:** An employee tries to access a sensitive document from a laptop in a different city.
- **The Response:** Because the network is no longer in a single physical office, the security system must verify the user's identity using **MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)** and check if their device is authorized before granting access.
