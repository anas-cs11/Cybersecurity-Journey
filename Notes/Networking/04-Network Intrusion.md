## The Importance of Network Security

This section explores why organizations invest heavily in securing their networks. Because networks are the backbone of modern business, they are constant targets for **Malicious Hackers** who want to steal data or stop operations.

---

## 1. Common Network Intrusion Attacks

Attackers use several methods to break into or disrupt a network. Understanding these "tools of the trade" is the first step in defending against them.

| **Attack Type** | **Simple Definition** | **Goal of the Attacker** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Malware** | "Malicious Software" designed to infect servers or computers. | To steal data, spy on users, or damage files. |
| **Spoofing** | Faking an identity (like an **IP** or **MAC** address) to look like a trusted source. | To trick the network into letting the attacker in. |
| **Packet Sniffing** | Using tools to "listen" to or capture data as it travels across the network. | To steal sensitive info like passwords or credit card numbers. |
| **Packet Flooding** | Overwhelming a network with too much data at once. | To crash the network and stop people from using it (**Disruption**). |

## 2. The Impact of a Network Breach

If a network is not secured, even one successful attack can have a "catastrophic" (disastrous) impact on a company.

- **Information Leakage:** Valuable or confidential data (like trade secrets or customer lists) can be stolen.
- **Reputation Damage:** Customers lose trust in the company, which hurts **Customer Retention** (keeping customers).
- **Financial Loss:** Fixing the attack and paying for legal issues costs a huge amount of money and time.

---

## 3. Real-World Context: The Home Depot Breach

**Case Study: The 2014 Retail Attack**
One of the most famous examples of a network failure happened to the American store, **Home Depot**.

1. **The Attack:** Hackers successfully broke into the company's network and infected their **Servers** with **Malware**.
2. **The Result:** Before the network administrators could stop the attack, the hackers stole the credit and debit card information of **56 million customers**.
3. **The Lesson:** This shows that once an attacker is "inside" a network, they can move quickly to steal massive amounts of private data.

## 4. Why We Secure Networks

Securing a network is not just about technology; it is about protecting the **Confidentiality**, **Integrity**, and **Availability** of information. Without these protections, a business cannot safely function in the digital world.
