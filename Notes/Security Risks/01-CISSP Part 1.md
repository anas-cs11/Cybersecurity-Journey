## **CISSP Security Domains (1-4)**

## **Core Concept: Security Posture**

- **Definition:** An organization’s ability to manage its defense of critical assets/data and react to changes.
- **Purpose of Domains:** To organize daily tasks, identify security gaps, and prevent negative consequences.

---

## **Domain 1: Security and Risk Management**

**Focus:** High-level oversight, goals, and legal compliance.

- **Security Goals:** Defining objectives to protect critical assets and **PII** (Personally Identifiable Information).
- **Risk Mitigation:** Implementing rules and procedures to reduce the impact of threats (e.g., a data breach).
- **Compliance:** Using internal policies, laws, and industry standards to guide security.
- **Business Continuity:** Creating disaster recovery plans so the company stays productive during a crisis.
- **Legal/Ethics:** Following worldwide regulations and ethical behavior to prevent fraud or negligence.
    - *Example:* A company creating a "Remote Work Policy" to ensure all employees follow the same security laws regardless of their location.

---

## **Domain 2: Asset Security**

**Focus:** Protecting physical and digital "stuff" (data, hardware, etc.).

- **Data Lifecycle:** Managing how data is **stored, maintained, retained, and destroyed**.
- **Access Control:** Knowing exactly what data exists and who is allowed to see it.
- **Handling Sensitive Info:** Ensuring PII or **SPII** (Sensitive PII) is safe on computers, over the internet, or in physical files.
    - *Example:* A security analyst overseeing the physical shredding of old hard drives so a hacker cannot recover old files from a dumpster.

---

## **Domain 3: Security Architecture and Engineering**

**Focus:** Building and optimizing the systems and tools that protect the company.

- **Shared Responsibility:** The idea that *every* employee (not just the IT team) plays a role in maintaining security.
- **Reporting Culture:** Policies that encourage users to spot and report suspicious activity immediately.
    - *Example:* Setting up a "Report Phishing" button in every employee's email inbox to catch malicious links early.

---

## **Domain 4: Communication and Network Security**

**Focus:** Securing the "pipes" that data travels through (Wi-Fi, Cloud, Cables).

- **Network Defense:** Keeping communications safe whether the employee is in the office, using the cloud, or working remotely.
- **Channel Management:** Controlling how devices connect to each other to prevent vulnerabilities.
    - *Example:* A security team disabling "Public Wi-Fi" or "Bluetooth" on company laptops to prevent hackers from intercepting data at a coffee shop.
