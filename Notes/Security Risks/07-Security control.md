## **Security Controls: The Specific Safeguards**

## **1. What are Security Controls?**

- **Definition:** Specific technical or physical protections designed to reduce a **particular** risk.
- **Goal:** To stop incidents like trespassing, fake account creation, or data theft.

---

## **2. Encryption (Protecting Data)**

- **The Process:** Converting readable data (**Plaintext**) into an unreadable, scrambled format (**Ciphertext**).
- **Decryption:** The only way to turn ciphertext back into readable plaintext (usually requiring a digital "key").
- **Purpose:** Ensures **Confidentiality**—even if a hacker steals the data, they cannot read it.
    - *Example:* Encrypting a customer’s Social Security Number so it looks like random gibberish to anyone without the key.

---

## **3. Authentication (Verifying Identity)**

- **Definition:** Proving that a person (or system) is who they claim to be.
- **Basic Level:** Username + Password (proving you *know* something).
- **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Requiring two or more "factors" to log in.
    - **Knowledge:** Something you know (Password/PIN).
    - **Possession:** Something you have (Security code sent to your phone).
    - **Inherence (Biometrics):** Something you *are* (Fingerprint, eye scan, or face ID).

---

## **4. Authorization (Granting Permission)**

- **Definition:** Determining what a person is **allowed to do** after they have been authenticated.
- **Purpose:** Ensuring people only see the data necessary for their specific job.
    - *Example:* A security analyst might be **authorized** to access private internal data (Deep Web), while a regular visitor is not.

---

## **5. New Threat: Vishing**

- **Definition:** Voice + Phishing. Using phone calls or voice technology to trick people into giving up secrets.
- **Biometric Risk:** Attackers can use vishing to impersonate a person's voice to bypass security or steal an identity
