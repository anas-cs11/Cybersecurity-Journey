## **The CIA Triad: Core Security Model**

## **1. Confidentiality (Keep it Secret)**

- **Definition:** Ensuring that only **authorized** people can see specific data.
- **The "Need to Know" Basis:** Data is hidden from everyone except those who actually need it to do their jobs.
- **Analyst Goal:** Use tools like encryption and access cards to stop unauthorized viewing.
    - *Bank Example:* A bank must ensure that a stranger cannot see your balance or transaction history.

## **2. Integrity (Keep it Accurate)**

- **Definition:** Ensuring that data is **correct, authentic, and reliable**. It hasn't been changed by a hacker or a system error.
- **Trust Factor:** As an analyst, you must decide if data can be trusted. If it has been tampered with, it has lost its integrity.
    - *Bank Example:* If your account suddenly shows a $1,000 purchase in a different country, the bank suspects the data's **integrity** is compromised and may freeze the account to verify it's really you.

## **3. Availability (Keep it Accessible)**

- **Definition:** Ensuring that data and systems are **up and running** for authorized users when they need them.
- **Reliability:** If a system crashes or is locked by ransomware, it is "unavailable," which stops people from working.
- **Analyst Goal:** Monitor networks and applications to ensure they are functioning properly and providing timely access.
    - *Bank Example:* A bank must ensure its website and mobile app are running 24/7 so customers can pay bills or check their balances at any time.

---

## **How the Triad Protects Against Threats**

By keeping the CIA Triad in mind, you can defend against the threats we discussed earlier:

- **Malware/Ransomware:** Attacks the **Availability** of your data by locking it.
- **Social Engineering/Phishing:** Attacks **Confidentiality** by tricking you into giving away secrets.
- **Data Theft:** Impacts **Confidentiality** (leaking data) and potentially **Integrity** (changing records)
