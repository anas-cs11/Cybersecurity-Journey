## **Internal Security Audit: Execution & Reporting**

Before finishing the audit, an analyst must ask: *What are we trying to achieve? Which assets are at risk? Are our current defenses enough?*

## **1. Controls Assessment**

This involves reviewing an organization's assets and checking if the current "locks" are actually working. Analysts group these into three main categories: 

| **Control Type** | **Definition** | **Examples** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Administrative** | The "Human" side; rules and policies. | Password policies, employee training, hiring procedures. |
| **Technical** | Hardware and software solutions. | **Encryption**, Firewalls, and **Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)**. |
| **Physical** | Real-world barriers to keep people out. | Security cameras, door locks, and ID badge scanners. |

## **2. Assessing Compliance**

Compliance means following the **laws** that protect data. If a company fails an audit here, they can face massive fines.

- **GDPR:** Required if the company does business in the European Union (EU).
- **PCI DSS:** Required if the company accepts credit card payments.
    - *Analyst Task:* Check if the company’s current data handling matches these legal requirements.

---

## **3. Communicating Results**

Once the "detective work" is done, you must share the findings with **stakeholders** (managers/owners). A good audit report includes:

- **Summary:** A quick look at the scope and goals.
- **Risk List:** What threats were found and **how fast** they need to be fixed (Priority).
- **Compliance Gaps:** Which laws the company is currently breaking.
- **Recommendations:** Clear steps to improve the organization's **security posture**.

---

## **Real-World Example: The Password Audit**

An analyst team conducts an audit and finds many employees are using weak passwords (like "123456").

- **The Result:** The compliance team uses this "evidence" to enforce a new policy requiring longer, more complex passwords.
- **The Value:** The audit identified a "gap" and fixed it before a hacker could exploit it.
