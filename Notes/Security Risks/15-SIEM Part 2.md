## **1. Types of SIEM Deployments**

Organizations choose how to host their **SIEM (Security Information and Event Management)** based on how much control they need versus how much hardware they want to manage.

| **Type** | **How it Works** | **Best For...** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Self-Hosted** | Installed on the company's own physical servers. Managed by their own IT (Information Technology) team. | Organizations that need **total physical control** over their data. |
| **Cloud-Hosted** | Managed by a third-party vendor and accessed over the internet. | Organizations that don't want to buy or maintain their own servers. |
| **Hybrid** | A mix of both self-hosted and cloud-hosted tools. | Companies that want cloud flexibility but need to keep some data on-site. |

## **2. Industry-Leading SIEM Tools**

There are three major versions of these tools that dominate the professional landscape:

## **Splunk (Enterprise & Cloud)**

Splunk is a massive data analysis platform.

- **Splunk Enterprise:** The **self-hosted** version. It searches and analyzes log data in real-time to provide alerts.
- **Splunk Cloud:** The **cloud-hosted** version. It’s perfect for "hybrid" companies that have some services on-site and some in the cloud.

## **Google Chronicle (Cloud-Native)**

Chronicle is a **cloud-native** tool, meaning it was built from the ground up specifically to live in the cloud.

- **Key Benefit:** It uses "cloud computing" to be incredibly fast and **scalable** (it can handle massive amounts of data without slowing down).
- **Functions:** It provides log monitoring, data analysis, and long-term data retention.

---

## **3. Cloud-Hosted vs. Cloud-Native**

While they sound the same, there is a small difference:

- **Cloud-Hosted:** A traditional tool that was "moved" to the internet.
- **Cloud-Native:** A tool designed specifically to use the internet's full power for **Availability** (always on) and **Flexibility** (easy to change).
