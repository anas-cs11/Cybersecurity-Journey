## **Ransomware: The Digital Lock**

- **Definition:** A type of malicious software (malware) that **encrypts** (locks) an organization’s data.
- **The Goal:** Threat actors demand a "ransom" payment in exchange for a **decryption key** (the digital password to unlock the files).
- **The Effect:** * Freezes network systems.
    - Makes devices unusable.
    - Stops normal business operations.

## **2. The Three Layers of the Web**

The web is like an iceberg; most of it is hidden beneath the surface.

- **Surface Web:** The part everyone uses daily (Social media, shopping, news). Accessible via standard browsers (Google, Bing).
- **Deep Web:** Parts of the web that require **authorization** to enter.
    - *Example:* A company’s private internal website (Intranet) or your personal Gmail inbox.
- **Dark Web:** Can only be accessed using **special software**.
    - **Note:** It is popular with criminals because it offers high secrecy. This is where stolen data is often sold or ransom negotiations happen.

---

## **3. Three Key Impacts of Security Incidents**

When a threat (like ransomware) hits a vulnerability, it affects the organization in three main ways:

## **A. Financial Impact**

- **Lost Productivity:** Work stops while systems are down.
- **Repair Costs:** High costs to hire experts to fix the breach.
- **Fines:** Penalties for breaking laws or safety regulations.

## **B. Identity Theft**

- **Data Risk:** Storing customer or employee info (PII) is a massive responsibility.
- **The Black Market:** Stolen PII is often sold on the **dark web** where hackers can avoid the law.

## **C. Reputation Damage**

- **Loss of Trust:** Customers may leave for a competitor if they feel their data isn't safe.
- **Bad Press:** Negative news coverage can cause permanent damage to a brand's image.
- **Legal Trouble:** Beyond reputation, losing data can lead to lawsuits.
