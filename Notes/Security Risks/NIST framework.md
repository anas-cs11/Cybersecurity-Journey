## NIST 
The NIST Cybersecurity Framework (NIST CSF) is a structured set of guidelines designed to help organizations identify, manage, reduce, and respond to cybersecurity risks.

It was developed by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), a U.S.-based standards organization, to provide a common language and systematic approach for cybersecurity risk management.

The framework is widely used by businesses, government agencies, educational institutions, and critical infrastructure sectors to improve their security posture and strengthen protection against cyber threats.

## **The 7 Steps of the NIST RMF**

## **Step 1: Prepare**

- **What it is:** Getting ready **before** a breach happens.
- **Your Role:** Identifying which security tools (controls) are needed to lower risks.

## **Step 2: Categorize**

- **What it is:** Organizing systems based on how much damage a hit to their **CIA Triad** (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability) would cause.
- **Your Role:** Following the specific rules your company has for protecting high-value assets, like customer data.

## **Step 3: Select**

- **What it is:** Choosing and documenting the specific "playbooks" or rules to protect the organization.
- **Your Role:** Keeping these playbooks and documents up-to-date so the team can react fast.

## **Step 4: Implement**

- **What it is:** Putting the plans into action.
- **Example:** If employees keep forgetting passwords, you might **implement** a new, easier-to-use password requirement or a reset tool.

## **Step 5: Assess**

- **What it is:** Checking the work. Does the security tool actually do what it’s supposed to do?
- **Your Role:** Finding weaknesses in the current tools and suggesting better ways to manage risk.

## **Step 6: Authorize**

- **What it is:** Taking "ownership" of the security state. Higher-ups decide if the remaining risks are acceptable.
- **Your Role:** Creating reports and project plans that show how security matches the company’s goals.

## **Step 7: Monitor**

- **What it is:** The daily "watchtower" work. Checking how systems are running in real-time.
- **Your Role:** Making sure current systems are still working correctly and supporting the company's safety.
