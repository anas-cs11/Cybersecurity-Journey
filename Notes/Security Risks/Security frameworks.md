## **Security Frameworks: The Organization's Blueprint**

## **1. What is a Security Framework?**

- **Definition:** Professional guidelines used to build plans that reduce risks and threats to data and privacy.
- **The "Starting Point":** Organizations don't start from scratch; they use frameworks (like NIST) to create their own specific **policies** and **processes**.
- **Coverage:** They protect against both digital threats (Ransomware) and human threats (Social Engineering).

---

## **2. Virtual vs. Physical Security**

Frameworks remind us that security isn't just about code; it’s about the environment.

- **Virtual Space:** Protecting data, networks, and software.
- **Physical Space:** Keeping the actual workplace safe.
    - *Example:* Requiring a **key card** or **ID badge** to enter the office building.

---

## **3. The Human Factor: Addressing the "Biggest Threat"**

Because people are often the weakest link in security, frameworks focus heavily on **Education and Awareness**.

- **Recognizing "Red Flags":** Training employees to spot signs of a potential attack (like a suspicious email).
- **Reporting Procedures:** Having a clear, fast plan for employees to report security issues before they spread.
- **Phishing Defense:** Creating specific response plans for when an employee accidentally clicks a malicious link.

---

## **4. Role as an Analyst**

- **Implementation:** You don't just learn the plan; you help put it into action.
- **Protection:** Your goal is to keep three groups safe:
    1. The **Organization** (Assets and Finances).
    2. The **Co-workers** (Privacy and Safety).
    3. The **People it Serves** (Customer Data).
